Didiér Stijn

Stijn started DJ ing in 1984, playing mostly disco oriented music at small school parties.

The release of Ten City’s ‘That’s the way love is’ in 1989, made him decide to change his musical style towards (deep) house music, a style he hasn’t left since. While for his first residency in a student club (1990-1996), he still combined house and disco, later on he focussed on UK/US house with a growing number of bookings for VIP rooms, gay parties and small Amsterdam based clubs.

Outdoor festivals have had a special place in his DJ career, having played New frontier (Holland’s first overnight outdoor festival) and several Queensday parties. His big breakthrough was also outdoors, at a Beachbop party at Woodstock beach club, where he played his soulful music for 4.000 music lovers. After that his career took off, playing Lowlands (Hollands second biggest pop festival) and receiving bookings to warm up for Roger Sanchez, St. Germain, Miguel Migs, DJ Deep, Jori Hulkkonen, Grant Nelson and Mark Doyle (Hed Kandi). He has (had) residencies in renowned Dutch clubs like Club Stalker, the Power Zone, Chemistry’s Housewarmin’ night, Club More, Paradiso, beachclub Republiek, and ID&T radio. Although he has adjusted his style somewhat throughout the years, he still plays the same kind of music he started out with years ago; warm, soulful, percussive and vocal house, with an occasional classic here and there.
