KRAAK & SMAAK: nieuw album

Kraak & Smaak drop their funk-fuelled debut artist album, ‘Boogie Angst’ through London’s funky breakbeat -bandits Jalapeno Records at the back end of June, via Lowlands distribution in the Benelux Oscar de Jong, Mark Kneppers and Wim Plug are Kraak & Smaak, a Dutch trio championing the sound of funked-up breaks, between them they whilst winning acclaim from the Radio, Club Jocks and producers around the globe.

Kraak & Smaak have a residency in 013, Tilburg, a position on the roster of Manchester’s highly acclaimed Best Kept Secret Agency and an Australian tour in the making as well as a steady stream of remix requests. Kraak & Smaak look set to imprint their Leiden based madness firmly into the year 2005 & Beyond with their live performance which they are currently rehearsing for. Kraak & Smaak have also been regularly been dropping remixes of the likes of Mark Rae, Max Sedgley, Karl Moestl amongst other and have produced a track for Wipneus & Pim, ‘Capitain Winokio’ as featured on the Compilation of Childrens Songs Part II.

To hear Kraak & Smaak in action whilst dropping their own dubplates twice on KONINGINNEDAG in Amsterdam check em out @ Â TWSTD BUITENPODIUM Weteringplantsoen, Amsterdam, 12:30 hrs.

Kraak & Smaak release the single ‘Money In The Bag’ through Jalapeno Records on 6th June, their debut album ‘Boogie Angst’ will follow end of June. (Lowlands in Benelux)

Between the three of them, Kraak & Smaak are inspired by the likes of The Plump DJs, Charles Webster, Madlib, Gill Scott Heron, James Lavelle, Miles Davis, Herbie Hancock and James Brown amongst others and cite the jazz, hip-hop, funk and breakbeat scenes as having an important impact on their music.

Mark is also one half of the retro 70s act Wipneus & Pim and a commercially successful DJ duo in Holland ‘We do a lot of festival stuff and we’ve got our own radio show too but I keep it totally separate from what I do with Kraak & Smaak’ he says.

Oscar is the main knob twiddler in the group and is currently studying at the Music Conservatory in Holland, ‘As a student I spend a lot of time alone listening to music but suddenly I realised I was pretty shit hot on the Fender Rhodes too. I think its made me want to get out there and perform more and with Kraak & Smaak I’ve got the perfect vehicle really’ smiles Oscar.

The third and final member is Wim has traversed the length and breadth of Holland and found himself up on the decks next to Portishead’s unofficial crate-digger Andy Smith, Depth Charge’s Saul Kane and drum ‘n’ bass DJ Aphrodite amongst many others. Adding to Wim’s colourful DJ CV is his multimedia turntable orchestra, ‘Polyvinyl Big Band’ which has performed at various music, film and art festivals.
