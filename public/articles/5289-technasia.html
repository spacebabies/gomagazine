Technasia

Technasia is a collaboration between Amil Khan, label manager based in Hong Kong, and Charles Siegling, musician and DJ based in Paris, France.

Having met in Hong Kong some years back, they felt it was time to put South East Asia on the world map in terms of the production of electronic dance music. They have been exposed to a variety of electronic music from abroad and have been influenced in their works by early electronic sounds from the likes of the Yellow Magic Orchestra, Coil, Xymox, and Depeche Mode…

Technasia have built their notoriety with acclaimed tracks such as “Descent”, “Future Mix” and “Hydra” and more recently “Force”, “Evergreen” and “Final Quadrant”. They have been supported by a wide variety of top international DJ/producers inclusing Laurent Garnier, Dave Clarke, Jeff Mills, Derrick May, Carl Cox and Sven Vath to name a few.

In 2001, Technasia released in Europe their first album "Future Mix" featuring their popular Technasia singles, as well as new and previously unreleased tracks. Previously released in Asia in July 2000, the album topped the Japanese national charts (#26). This release has lead Technasia to win three award trophies at the 2001 Edition of Berlin's Musik and Machine Awards : Best Album ("Future Mix"), Best Single ("Force") and Best Newcomer. The album acted as a tribute to the pioneering Chinese radio show of the same name, based on the same concept that featured excepts and jingles from the actual radio show itself! It was presented by Charles Siegling, and a Chinese local radio DJ, Michael Nee, from 1998 to 2001 to the entire South East China province of Guangdong (Hong Kong, Canton, Shenzhen, Foshan�?�) covering 60 million people population.
Since 1999, Technasia diversified its activities by launching two new record labels. The first one, Sino, dedicated to welcoming fellow artists such as Kelli Hand, Renato Cohen, John Thomas, DJ Skull, Steve Rachmad (aka Sterac) and John Tejada... The second one, Star Tracks, Charles Siegling's solo project, a five-releases-only DJ club tools label, available worldwide since December 2000.
In 2001, Technasia toured as a Live set taking them to leading events and clubs worldwide. Charles Siegling has also started several DJ residencies in popular electronic music clubs such as Fuse in Brussels and Nitsa in Barcelona amongst others.
