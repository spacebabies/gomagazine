PARADISOUL, FRIDAY MAY 14th

Grandmaster Flash, one of hip-hop’s founding fathers and the creator of the Quick Mix, was the first person to change the arrangements of songs by using duplicate copies of records and manually editing/repeating the climatic part (later called the “break”) by rubbing the record back and forth. This became known as “cutting”, which was later called “scratching”.
