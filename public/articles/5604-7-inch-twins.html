7 inch twins

The 7 Inch Twins make the 80’s happen again in the 21st century. Spelen Friday 21 December op 80s Verantwoord.

Their dancenight is called ‘Eighties verantwoord’ (this is a hard to explain Dutch language joke). Joop (right) and Robert play original 7 inch vinyl singles, connecting ska with reggae, pop with disco, rock with acid house and new wave with Dutch classics. Their policy is to play danceable, recognizable music and to try to please everybody. The people on the dancefloor can interact with the dj’s by requesting by mouth or with help of the terminal on the side of the dancefloor and videoscreen. Or they can send requests in advance to [email protected] 7 Inch Twins are able to meet with almost any request, because they have a private collection of 4000 7 inch singles. And still growing….

Since April 2000, the 7 Inch Twins have had a residency in Ekko, Utrecht, where they make the crowd go crazy the first Friday of each month. The night always sells out in presale. Due to the succes, the first Friday was shared with Tivoli every other month from January 2004 on. During the Eighties verantwoord night, everybody can find out how we were once brainwashed by the most wrong, funniest and beautiful songs. Every month, Ekko and Tivoli are filled with crazy relics. There are also various side activities (provided by Triomf), like vj’ing with 100% classic 80’s footage, old skool computer games or race track and special acts for larger venues (dancers, aerobic, styling plus models etc). Baltimora’s create the soundtrack to the 80’s lounge in the Ekko cafe and occasionally they present an 80’s popquiz.

Since October 2002, 7 Inch Twins, Triomf and Baltimora’s have hit clubs outside Utrecht (Patronaat, Boerderij, Doornroosje, Panama, Ruigoord, Mezz, Timboektoe, Boulevard and Paard van Troje), following the succes 7 Inch Twins had on the dance stage of Parkpop 2002 and Golf @ Lowlands 2002. In 2004, 80’s Verantwoord can be finally enjoyed to the max in Amsterdam: each 3rd Friday in Hotel Arena Tonight (’s Gravesandestraat 51), with 7 Inch Twins in the main room and Baltimora’s spinning mainly disco, reggae and other forgotten dancefloor crackers in the 2nd room.
