Kraak & Smaak album expected Summer 2005

For those that don’t know Kraak & Smaak is not the reason your little sister stays out all weekend but are in fact three Leiden based Dutch guys who are single-handedly putting Holland on the GFPS. That?s the Global Funk Positioning System for those that don’t know. A strange combination of one half of WipNeus & Pim (we’ll leave you guessing as to which), a record shop owner (Velvet, Leiden…plug plug) & University lecturer.

2004 has seen highly acclaimed DJ appearances I Holland The Supernatural Festival, Per?s Earth the Melkweg, City Sounds Festival in Tilburg, Paard in Den Haag amongst others. The Kraak & Smaak album is expected to drop early in the Summer of 2005

Now Kraak & Smaak are about to serve their 4th serving of funk  ‘Say Yeah EP’?: ‘Say Yeah’ / ‘Maputo Express’ / ‘Danse Macabre’ (JAL 28WL) – Intergroove (UK) / Lowlands (NL/B) release date: 17.01.05 * A1- the fantastic Say Yeah is classic K&S with a deliberately old school vibe bringing together legendary funk samples (no it?s not JB) and 2K+ production values. ?Lemme hear ya say yeah?  our party starter shouts ?say it again? he screams and all that’s left to do is lay back and watch the dancefloor catch fire. * B1 is Maputo Express ? a rolling tribal track with an African vocal vibe that seems to be finding favour in both the house and breaks camps. * Finally B2 Danse Macabre shows the often ignored side of Kraak & Smaak’s work with a beautiful downbeat soundscape with a softly sung haunting jazz vocal. Nice. (for audio clip scroll down to end of page)

So far Kraak & Smaak have served up 3 tasty little morsels:  - 'Money in the Bag EP'? - limited promos, featured on playlists in the UK: Annie Nightingale (BBC Radio One), Ali B (Capital FM London + listed for four weeks in Club Chart), Australia (Groove Train, ABC Radio), Germany (techno.de), Austria, Latvia, Spain (Clubbingspain.com), Hungary (DJ Palotai), USA (Ursula 1000, Thunderball Soundsystem), Canada (Torontobreaks; Dylan aka The Phat Conductor), Netherlands (VPRO, 3voor12 ), Switzerland, etc.
- 'Keep On Searching EP'? - featured on playlists of amongst others Pete Tong (Essential Selection), Lottie, Seb Fontaine, Blue Room, Annie Nightingale, (BBC Radio 1), Solid Steel (Coldcut / Ninja Tune), etc. BBC 'dance tune of the week'?, was listed in Ministry of Sound's 'Vital Vinyl? chart and DMC chart.
- 'Set Fire To The Disco EP'? - better take an extinguisher to the centre of the floor.'everything is plastic' is explosive (quote taken from Patrick Forge?s playlist) - BBC Single of The week, DJ support from: Mark Rae, Nik Weston, Patrick Forge, Laurent Garnier, Tom Findlay (Groove Armada), James Hyman (XFM), Nico de Ceglia (M.O.S. & The End). Radio Support: Annie Nightingale (Radio 1), Ali B (Capital FM), The Rinse (XFM), MOS Express (MOS radio), Deepblue (MOS Radio), C List MOS Radio, The Hook Up (Juice 107.2) a.o. Chart Positions: #12 Capital Club Chart.
